Design Directive 1

Proposed Email to PALS/DETNET/MPLS WGs

Dear PALS, DetNET and MPLS WGs,

The MPLS Open DT is working on a solution for carrying ancillary data in MPLS that will address several use cases. The MPLS Open DT has put together a number of design directives, also stated below:

MPLS Open DT Design Directives:

  • Existing specification and implementations for GAL/G-ACh and the "Control Word" specified by the DETNET WG (d-CW) will continue to operate as specified.
  • RFC 5586 "MPLS Generic Associated Channel" (including the updates by RFC 7274, RFC 6423, RFC 7214, and RFC 7026) will not be changed by the MPLS Open Design Team.
  • No changes should be made to the associated channel (ACH) by third parties, without careful coordination with what will be specified by the MPLS Open design team.
  • RFC 8964 will not be changed, and the DETNET Control Word (d-CW) will continue to operate as specified.
  • The MPLS Open Design Team will continue to define methods based on indicators (e.g. Forwarding Action Indicator (FAI) style SPL) and PSD (Post Stack Data)
  • A given MPLS label stack MUST NOT simultaneously be followed by more than one set of Post Stack Data (PSD).

That is, at most one of the new-style PSD, PW CW, d-CW, ACH may be present after the label whose Bottom of Stack bit (BoS) is set, in any given label stack. Thus, any changes in the format or processing of the GAL/G-Ach, PW CW or DETNET CW MUST be made in the context of the new PSD format.

  • If/when new methods are defined to indicate and use PSD, it is up to the specification of these new methods to address backwards and forwarding compatibility.

We are asking you to review these design directives and let us know if you have any feedback or concerns.

Signed, (for the MPLS Open DT)

2021-09-30

GAL/GACh, the DETNET Control Word (d-CW) and new methods to Indicate PSD (i.e. Ancillary Data after the BoS).


The following design directive has been issued for the MPLS Open Design Team by the MPLS wg co-chairs, and is in effect from 2021-09-30.

  • Existing specification and implementations for GAL/G-ACh and the "Control Word" specified by the DETNET wg (d-CW) will continue to operate as specified.
  • RFC 5586 "MPLS Generic Associated Channel" (including the updates by RFC 7274, RFC 6423, RFC 7214, and RFC 7026) will not be changed by the MPLS Open Design Team.
  • No changes should be made to the associated channel (ACH) by third parties, without careful coordination with what will be specified by the MPLS Open design team.
  • RFC 8964 will not be changed, and the DETNET Control Word (d-CW) will continue to operate as specified.
  • The Open design team will continue define methods based on indicators (e.g. FAI-style SPL) and PSD (Post Stack Data)

A given label stack cannot simultaneously be followed by more than one set of PSD.

I.e. the FAI-style PSD, d-CW, ACH or any other future PSD cannot be present simultaneously after the label carrying the Bottom of Stack bit (BoS) in any given label stack.

If/when new methods are defined to indicate and use PSD, it is up to the specification of these new methods to address backwards and forwarding compatibility.

Loa Andersson for MPLS co-chairs.
